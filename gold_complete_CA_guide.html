Price Drivers
What Moves Gold Prices?
💵
US Dollar Strength
Gold is priced globally in USD. When the dollar weakens, gold becomes cheaper for other countries, increasing demand and prices. Inverse correlation with USD index is approximately −0.75. Key factor for understanding price movement.
📈
Inflation & Real Interest Rates
Gold is the classic inflation hedge. When real interest rates (nominal − inflation) are negative, gold becomes the most attractive store of value. India's CPI inflation and RBI repo rate both affect domestic gold demand.
🌍
Geopolitical Risk
Wars, sanctions, and political instability trigger "flight to safety." Russia-Ukraine, Israel-Gaza, US-China tensions all drove gold up 25% in investment demand in 2024 (World Gold Council).
🏦
Central Bank Purchases
RBI and global central banks have been net buyers since 2010. India's RBI has been steadily increasing gold reserves. Central bank buying is now a floor for gold prices globally.
🪔
Indian Seasonal Demand
Akshaya Tritiya (April-May), Dhanteras (Oct-Nov), and wedding season (Nov-Feb) create predictable demand spikes in India. India accounts for ~22% of global gold consumption and is the world's second-largest consumer.
💱
Rupee vs Dollar
Even if global gold prices stay flat, a depreciating rupee increases domestic gold price in INR. This is why Indian gold prices sometimes rise even when international prices fall — pure currency effect.

CA Note: Gold Valuation for Loan Purpose
Banks calculate loan amount as: (Net weight after removing stones) × (Current IBJA spot rate) × (Purity %) × (LTV ratio). Stones, enamel, and non-gold parts are excluded from net weight. This is why a 50g gold necklace with heavy stone work may have a net gold weight of only 35g. Always inform clients to know the net gold weight before applying.
Global vs Domestic Price
How Domestic Gold Price is Calculated
1
International Spot Price (MCX/COMEX)
Global gold is quoted in USD/troy ounce on COMEX (New York). 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams. Current global price (~$3,100/oz as of March 2026) is the starting point.
2
Convert to INR/gram using spot FX
($3,100 × 85.5 USD/INR) ÷ 31.1035 grams = approximately ₹8,520/gram base rate (24K) before duties.
3
Add Import Duty + Customs (6% effective 2024)
India cut gold import duty from 15% to 6% in July 2024 Budget. Add customs levy + AIDC. This reduced domestic gold prices by ~₹4,500/10g immediately after the reduction.
4
IBJA Fixes the Rate Daily
India Bullion and Jewellers Association (IBJA) fixes the standard rate daily which banks and NBFCs use for gold loan valuation. Banks use the previous day's IBJA rate for loan appraisal.
5
Retail Price = Base + GST 3% + Making Charges
When you buy jewellery: IBJA rate + 3% GST + making charges (₹300–₹1,000/gram). Making charges are a dead cost — not recovered on resale or loan. This is a critical client advisory point.

Sovereign Gold Bonds — Special Focus
SGB — Best Tax-Efficient Gold Investment
⚠️
Budget 2026 Change — Capital Gains Exclusion Now Restricted
The capital gains tax exemption on SGBs is now available only to original subscribers who held the bond continuously till maturity (8 years). Investors who buy SGBs from the secondary market are NOT eligible for the capital gains exemption even if they hold till maturity. Also, no new SGB tranches have been issued since 2024 and none are announced for FY 2026–27. Secondary market purchase at NSE/BSE remains an option but with different tax treatment.
🏛️

Tenure: 8 years (exit from 5th year on interest payment dates)

Capital gains at maturity: NIL (original subscribers only)

Chapter 03 · Gold Loan Mechanics
Gold Loans — Everything from A to Z
A gold loan is a secured loan where physical gold ornaments or bank-issued gold coins are pledged as collateral. The lender retains the gold, provides a loan against its value, and returns the gold upon full repayment. It is the fastest, cheapest, and least documentation-heavy loan in India.
⚡
Why Gold Loans are Unique
No credit score needed — secured by gold, not creditworthiness
No income proof — any individual 18+ qualifies
Fastest disbursal — 30 minutes to 2 hours
Lowest interest — 8.75% (SBI) vs 12–18% personal loan
Foreclosure anytime — most banks zero prepayment penalty
CIBIL score improves — timely repayment builds credit history
📊
Loan to Value (LTV) — The Key Ratio
LTV = (Loan Amount ÷ Gold Market Value) × 100

RBI Mandated LTV Caps (Updated 2026):
Loans ≤ ₹2.5 lakh → 85% LTV
Loans ₹2.5L–₹5L → 80% LTV
Loans > ₹5 lakh → 75% LTV

Example: Gold worth ₹10L → Max loan = ₹8.5L (if loan ≤₹2.5L), or ₹7.5L (if >₹5L)
🏦
Banks vs NBFCs — Key Difference
Banks (SBI, Canara, Union): Lower rate (8.75–10%), more documentation, slower, better regulated, gold held in vault

NBFCs (Muthoot, Manappuram): Higher rate (12–24%), faster (15 min), minimal docs, more branches in rural areas, branch auctions on default more common

CA Advice: Always use banks for amounts above ₹2L. NBFCs only for genuine emergencies under ₹50K.

Step-by-Step Process
How to Apply — Exact Process
1
Pre-Visit Preparation
Weigh your gold at home (approximate). Separate stone-studded pieces (stones will be excluded). Collect Aadhaar, PAN, 2 photos. Call SBI branch: 1800-1234 or SMS "GOLD" to 7208933145 to confirm OD scheme availability at your branch. Check if your branch does gold appraisal in-house or outsources — in-house is faster.
2
Visit Branch + Gold Appraisal
Walk into any SBI branch with your gold and documents. The bank's certified gold appraiser will test purity using an XRF machine (touchstone test at some branches). They calculate: net gold weight (excluding stones) × purity % × IBJA rate × LTV ratio = your eligible loan amount. The appraiser's certificate is generated in the system immediately.
3
Specifically Request the OD Scheme
Tell the bank officer: "I want the Liquid Gold Loan / Gold Loan Overdraft scheme for 36 months, not the term loan." Many branch officers default to the standard term loan. You must specifically ask for OD. This is crucial — it is the only way to avoid the annual full repayment problem you experienced.
4
Alternatively, Apply via YONO App (Zero Processing Fee)
If you are an SBI customer, download the YONO app. Go to Loans → Gold Loan. Apply online. Processing fee is ZERO when applied through YONO. However, you still must visit the branch to physically deposit the gold for appraisal. The app just pre-fills your application and saves the fee (₹750–₹1,500).
5
Loan Sanction + OD Account Opening
After appraisal, a Gold Loan OD account (overdraft account) is opened. The sanctioned limit is credited to this account. You can withdraw using cheque, net banking, or ATM (if OD-linked debit card provided). Interest is applied monthly on the amount drawn. Gold is stored safely in the branch vault.
6
Managing the OD Account — Smart Usage
• Withdraw only what you need — interest is charged only on used amount
• Deposit surplus money back into OD account to reduce interest liability
• Pay monthly interest via YONO or auto-debit from SB account
• Drawing power may change if gold market price falls significantly — bank may call for additional gold or partial repayment if LTV breaches 75%
• Keep a buffer — don't draw 100% of sanctioned limit
7
At Maturity (36 months)
Repay the principal in full. Bank returns your gold within 7 working days (mandatory per RBI 2025). If you want to continue, take a fresh gold loan at that time — current gold value will likely be higher, meaning you can get more loan. No renewal needed in between — only one repayment event in 3 years.
✅
Can You Switch Your Existing Term Loan to OD?
Yes, but it requires closing the existing term loan first. Process:
1. Arrange the principal amount (or take a personal/family loan temporarily)
2. Close the existing SBI term gold loan — get gold released
3. Immediately re-pledge the same gold under the Liquid Gold Loan (OD) scheme
4. New OD account opened — 36-month term, interest-only monthly payment
The entire process can be done at the branch in a single day. The cost is one processing fee (₹750 + GST or zero via YONO) and gold appraisal charge.
Chapter 05 · Problem Solver
All Common Problems & Exact Solutions
Why it happens: RBI 2025 mandate — all banks must require full principal + interest repayment before re-pledging gold. This is not negotiable at bank level.

Solutions in order of ease:
1. Switch to OD scheme — apply for Liquid Gold Loan at SBI (36 months, interest only monthly) → no renewal for 3 years
2. Partial repayment strategy: Pay ₹1L, bank releases proportionate gold, re-pledge that gold for fresh ₹1L loan → cycle through
3. Bridge loan: Take 1-month personal loan from family/friend, repay gold loan, get gold, immediately re-pledge under OD scheme. Personal loan for 1 month = negligible interest
4. Employer advance: Request salary advance to bridge the gap
5. Switch banks: Close loan at current bank, same day open OD at Canara (Swarna OD — 24 months) or SBI OD
Why it happens: Your drawing power in OD = current gold value × LTV%. If gold price drops 10%, your drawing power drops proportionally. If your current usage exceeds new drawing power, bank issues a margin call.

Solutions:
1. Deposit more gold to restore drawing power (most common and easiest)
2. Partial repayment to bring usage within new drawing power limits
3. Do nothing — if price drop is temporary and you expect recovery, you can request 30-day grace period from branch manager

CA Note: Monitor gold price. If MCX gold drops more than 15%, proactively add collateral rather than waiting for bank notice.
How gold loan interest is calculated:
• Term loans: Simple interest on principal, calculated daily, charged monthly
• OD accounts: Daily product method — interest = (outstanding balance × rate) ÷ 365

Formula for 1 month at 9%:
₹3,00,000 × 9% ÷ 12 = ₹2,250 per month

Common confusion: If you have a ₹3L OD but only used ₹2L, interest = ₹2L × 9% ÷ 12 = ₹1,500. Banks charge on USED amount in OD, not the sanctioned limit. This is the key advantage of OD over term loan.

Request: Ask branch for a "loan statement" showing daily product to verify calculation. Banks are obligated to provide this.
Your Rights under RBI 2025 Guidelines:
1. 14-day advance written notice mandatory before auction (new rule)
2. Right to attend gold valuation — you cannot be excluded
3. Right to multiple bidders — auction must be transparent
4. Surplus return within 7 days — if auction proceeds exceed outstanding dues
5. Shortfall recovery — if gold auction doesn't cover dues, bank can come after you for balance

What to do immediately:
1. Do NOT ignore notices — respond in writing
2. Approach branch manager immediately and request OTS (One Time Settlement)
3. Get a legal notice from an advocate if bank is not following proper procedure
4. File RBI complaint at: rbi.org.in → Complaint Management System (CMS)
Responsibility: Bank is a bailee and is legally responsible for the safety of pledged gold. Any loss or damage is the bank's liability.

Actions:
1. Document at pledge time — get a receipt with description (weight, karat, design, photos)
2. If damage found at release: DO NOT sign the release receipt until discrepancy is noted in writing
3. File written complaint with branch manager + Regional Manager + Banking Ombudsman
4. Bank must compensate at current market value for any loss

Prevention: Click photos of your gold before depositing. Get the receipt carefully checked — note item descriptions, weight, and karat clearly.
Why this happens: Some branch staff are not aware of all schemes, or branches have internal targets for term loans vs OD.

Solutions:
1. Ask to speak to the Branch Manager directly
2. Reference: "Liquid Gold Loan" — this is the official SBI product name
3. Show the SBI official page: sbi.co.in → Personal Banking → Loans → Gold Loan → "Liquid Gold Loan"
4. Apply via YONO app — you can select the scheme type online, forcing the branch to process it
5. Try a different SBI branch — larger city branches have more scheme awareness
6. Call SBI Contact Centre: 1800-1234 and get the scheme confirmed before visiting

STRIDHAN Limits — Gold Without Questions (CBDT Circular)
As per CBDT Circular No. 1994 (still valid), the following gold jewellery held by household members is presumed to be legitimately held — no questions asked on income-tax raids or search:
• Married woman: 500 grams
• Unmarried woman: 250 grams
• Male member: 100 grams
Any gold above these limits must be supported by documented source — inheritance documents, purchase receipts, gift deeds, or ITR data. As a CA, ensure your clients maintain proper documentation for gold holdings above STRIDHAN limits.
